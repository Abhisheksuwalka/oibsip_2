Sushant Singh Rajput

Born on January 21, 1986

Sushant Singh Rajput was a true renaissance man, an Indian actor whose boundless curiosity and passion transcended the realms of cinema. While he etched his mark in Hindi films, his story was one of defying conventions and shattering barriers.

Rajput's journey began on the small screen, where he captivated audiences in popular serials like "Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil" and "Pavitra Rishta." But the silver screen beckoned, and he made a striking debut with "Kai Po Che!," earning a Filmfare nomination for Best Male Debut.

From there, Rajput's career was a kaleidoscope of versatility. He donned the hats of a romantic lead in "Shuddh Desi Romance," a sharp detective in "Detective Byomkesh Bakshy!," and even a supporting role that left an indelible impact in the satirical masterpiece "PK."

Yet, his crowning achievement came when he embodied the spirit of India's beloved cricketer, M.S. Dhoni, in the biopic "M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story." His transformative performance earned him a well-deserved Filmfare nomination for Best Actor.

Rajput's on-screen magic continued to weave its spell in hits like "Kedarnath" and "Chhichhore," but his true legacy extended far beyond the confines of the cinema. An astrophile, he pursued his passion for the cosmos by acquiring a Meade 14" LX600 telescope. A polyglot, he mastered multiple languages, including Hindi, English, Bengali, Greek, and Spanish, fueled by an insatiable thirst for knowledge.

His untimely demise in 2020 left a void in the hearts of millions, but his final film, "Dil Bechara," released posthumously, served as a poignant reminder of his incredible talent and the lasting impact he had on the world of entertainment and beyond.

Here's a time line of Sushant Singh Rajput's life :

1986

He took his first breath in Patna, the vibrant capital of Bihar, embarking on a journey that would captivate the world.

2002

Following his mother's tragic departure, his family relocated to Delhi, where Sushant Singh Rajput pursued his intermediate studies at Kulachi Hansraj Model School.

2006

During his fourth year of engineering, he made the bold decision to abandon his course, heeding the call of the arts and performance.

2008

Sushant Singh Rajput's talent caught the eye of casting directors, leading to an audition and his subsequent casting as the second lead in the television series "Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil."

2010

His exceptional performance earned him his first accolade, the Best Actor award at the Indian Television Academy Awards.

2010

He further cemented his status with three major television awards, including Best Male Actor and Most Popular Actor.

2011

Sushant Singh Rajput continued to captivate audiences in "Pavitra Rishta" from its inception in 2009 until his exit in 2011.

2011

Mukesh Chhabra, the casting director of "Kai Po Che!," recognized Sushant Singh Rajput's potential and invited him for an audition.

2013

"Kai Po Che!" hit the silver screen, becoming a commercial and critical success, solidifying Sushant Singh Rajput's transition to the big screen.

2014

After garnering critical acclaim in Bollywood, Sushant Singh Rajput made a special reappearance in the final episode of "Pavitra Rishta," the show that launched his career.

2014

His film "PK" shattered records, becoming the highest-grossing Indian film of that year.

2014

On September 25, 2014, Sushant Singh Rajput was announced as the actor chosen to portray the legendary cricketer Dhoni in the biopic "M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story," directed by Neeraj Pandey.

2015

"Detective Byomkesh Bakshy!" hit theaters on April 3, 2015, and while it underperformed at the box office, it received positive critical acclaim.

2016

"M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story," produced by Arun Pandey with Fox Star Studios, was released on October 2, 2016, showcasing Sushant Singh Rajput's remarkable transformation.

2017

His rising stardom earned him a spot on the prestigious Forbes India's Top 100 Celebrity list for the first time.

2017

"Raabta," his next cinematic venture, graced the silver screen on June 9, 2017.

2018

Sushant Singh Rajput made a cameo appearance in the comedic film "Welcome to New York," released on February 23, 2018.

2019

His romantic comedy "Chhichhore," produced by Fox Star Studios and Nadiadwala Grandson Entertainment, hit theaters on September 6, 2019, delighting audiences once again.

2020

"Dil Bechara" marked his final on-screen appearance, a poignant farewell to his devoted fans.

2020

Tragically, Sushant Singh Rajput's journey came to an abrupt end when he was found lifeless, hanging from the ceiling fan in his Bandra, Mumbai residence.
